Catharina Felke

Hi, my name is Catharina Felke. I am an investigative journalist focused on working with data, freedom of information requests and open source intelligence tools.

Currently, I am part of the Voting Access Team -- a yearlong investigation by Columbia Journalism Investigations, USA Today and Frontline. I graduated from the Lede Program for data journalism at Columbia University Graduate School of Journalism in 2018. Before moving to New York, I worked as an investigative reporter at the data unit of the German public network broadcasting, and at the investigative desk of Sueddeutsche Zeitung.

‘They should have done something’

The Milwaukee Journal Sentinel put out a call for voters in Wisconsin asking whether they had received their absentee ballot in time for the election in early April 2020. An election like no other, it took place as the state topped 2,500 confirmed coronavirus cases and nearly 100 deaths. We received more than 600 responses. Our reporting showed a system leaking from all sides, buckling under the weight of a global pandemic and partisan bickering that kept the logistics of election day up in the air until less than a day before polls opened.

The risk of unknown wells in Utah

The Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) struggles with identifying gas and oil wells across the country. Looking into the spread of wells in Utah I discovered that people with little income are more likely to live close to a possibly risky gas or oil well; schools are also affected.

The EPA keeps saying ‘No’

Through an analysis of FOIA reports published by the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) I showed the agency's new favorite exemption when it comes to denying information and whether more freedom of information requests were rejected under Donald Trump's or Barack Obama's first year as a president.

Complex surgeries, no supervision

We showed how negligent quality management in German hospitals endangers patients' safety by analyzing almost 6,000 documents and confronting more than 100 hospitals. This project was a joint investigation between the data unit and the health desk of the German public broadcaster; it was published online, as a radio and various TV features (read translation).
