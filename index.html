Catharina Felke

Hi, my name is Catharina Felke. I am an investigative journalist focused on working with data, freedom of information requests and open source intelligence tools.

Currently, I am based in New York City where I recently graduated from the Lede Program for data journalism at Columbia University Graduate School of Journalism. Before that, I worked as an investigative reporter at the data unit of the German public broadcaster.

The risk of unknown wells in Utah

The Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) struggles with identifying gas and oil wells across the country. Looking into the spread of wells in Utah I discovered that people with little income are more likely to live close to a possibly risky gas or oil well; schools are also affected.

The EPA keeps saying ‘No’

Through an analysis of FOIA reports published by the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) I showed the agency's new favorite exemption when it comes to denying information and whether more information law requests were rejected under president Donald Trump's or Barack Obama's first year.

Complex surgeries, no supervision

We showed how negligent quality managament in German hospitals endangers patients' safety by analyzing almost 6,000 documents and confronting more than 100 hospitals. This project was a joint investigation between the data unit and the health desk of the German public broadcaster; it was published online, as a radio and various TV features (read translation).
