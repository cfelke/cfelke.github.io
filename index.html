Catharina Felke

Hi, my name is Catharina Felke. I am an investigative journalist focused on working with freedom of information requests, data and doing digital research.

Currently, I'm mainly freelancing for the investigative unit at the German public broadcaster NDR. Before that, I was part of the Voter Access Team — an investigation into voter access with Columbia Journalism Investigations, USA Today and PBS Frontline — who received a 2021 George Foster Peabody Award for the documentary 'Whose Vote Counts'. In 2018, I graduated from the Lede Program for data journalism at Columbia University Graduate School of Journalism.

The Russian Asset Tracker

As part of an international collaboration I researched and reported on assets linked to a group of Russian oligarchs—the Navalny 35. Alexei Navalny, the jailed Russian opposition leader, had accused them of benefiting from the regime of Vladimir Putin. So far, we revealed assets worth more than $17 billion around the world. The project was initiated by the OCCRP and the Guardian, and is an ongoing investigation.

TikTok's shadowban in Germany

We revealed that TikTok has been disproportiontely banning words associated with the LGBTQI community in Germany; words related to Nazism as well as the name of Chinese tennis player Peng Shuai were also blocked. The company has subsequently removed some of the terms discovered in our research. I talked to DW News about our findings.

Covering voter access in 2020

Published front-page investigative and editorial work for USA Today, and its local affiliates like the Milwaukee Journal Sentinel. Moreover, I provided research and reporting for 'Whose Vote Counts', an award-winning documentary premiering on PBS Frontline October 20, 2020.
